Pre-Internet Era

Discover the innovations and key figures that paved the way for the modern internet.

The Foundations of the Internet

The pre-internet era, spanning the 1950s to the 1960s, marked a time of groundbreaking innovations and visionaries who laid the foundation for the interconnected world we experience today.

Key Moments

J.C.R. Licklider

Known for conceptualizing a globally interconnected network, Licklider envisioned what he called the "Intergalactic Computer Network," which served as the inspiration for today's internet.

The Cold War and the Need for Secure Communication

During the Cold War, the threat of nuclear attacks prompted the necessity for decentralized communication systems to ensure survival and coordination in critical scenarios.

ARPA and DARPA

The Advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA), established in 1958, played a pivotal role in developing advanced technologies, including early forms of what would become the internet. In later years, ARPA transitioned to DARPA, focusing on emerging technologies.

Packet Switching

Pioneers such as Paul Baran and Donald Davies developed packet-switching technologies, which became the foundation of modern data transmission systems.

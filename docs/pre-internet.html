Pre-Internet Era

JC Licklider

  • Who: J.C.R. Licklider, a computer scientist and psychologist.
  • What: He envisioned a global network of computers, calling it the "Intergalactic Computer Network".
  • Why: His vision was to allow humans to interact with computers in a way that facilitates communication and collaboration over large distances.
  • When: He proposed this idea in the early 1960s.
  • How: His work at ARPA (Advanced Research Projects Agency) laid the groundwork for ARPANET, the precursor to the internet.

Nuclear Weapons

  • Who: Governments and defense agencies.
  • What: The potential of nuclear war spurred the need for resilient communication systems.
  • Why: The need for secure, decentralized communication systems to withstand possible attacks during the Cold War.
  • When: This need arose during the height of Cold War tensions in the 1950s and 1960s.
  • How: Research funded by ARPA led to the development of packet-switching technology, which would become critical in ARPANET’s development.

ARPA (Later DARPA)

  • Who: ARPA (Advanced Research Projects Agency), later renamed DARPA (Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency).
  • What: ARPA funded critical research that led to the creation of ARPANET, the precursor to the modern internet.
  • Why: ARPA was created to fund advanced technologies for national defense purposes.
  • When: ARPA was founded in 1958, following the launch of Sputnik by the Soviet Union.
  • How: ARPA’s research included the development of packet-switching networks, which would later be used in ARPANET.

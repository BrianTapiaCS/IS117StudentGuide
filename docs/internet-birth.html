The Birth of the Internet

ARPANET

  • Who: ARPA (Advanced Research Projects Agency).
  • What: ARPANET was the first network to implement TCP/IP, the core protocol of the internet.
  • Why: It was developed to allow research institutions to communicate over long distances and share data securely.
  • When: ARPANET was created in 1969.
  • How: ARPANET used packet-switching technology to transmit data, and in 1983 it adopted TCP/IP, which allowed for interoperability between networks.

Vint Cerf & Bob Kahn

  • Who: Vint Cerf and Bob Kahn, computer scientists known as the “Fathers of the Internet.”
  • What: They developed the TCP/IP protocol, the foundation of data communication on the internet.
  • Why: They sought to create a reliable, standardized protocol for networks to communicate with each other.
  • When: TCP/IP was developed in the 1970s and implemented in 1983.
  • How: Their work allowed different types of networks to communicate seamlessly, transforming ARPANET into the global internet.

Early ARPANET Programs

  • Who: ARPA and early researchers on ARPANET.
  • What: Programs like Gopher and Telnet were used for information retrieval and remote access over ARPANET.
  • Why: These programs were some of the first to make ARPANET practical for academic and research use.
  • When: Early 1970s.
  • How: Gopher was an early search tool, while Telnet allowed users to log into remote systems.

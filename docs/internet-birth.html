The Birth of the Internet

ARPANET

  • What: ARPANET was the first network to implement the protocol suite TCP/IP, which became the foundation for the modern internet.
  • When: ARPANET was developed in the late 1960s, with the first successful message sent in 1969.
  • Why: The goal was to create a resilient communication network that could survive potential disruptions and ensure data could still be transferred.
  • Who: ARPANET was developed by the Advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA) of the U.S. Department of Defense.
  • How: By using packet-switching technology, ARPANET allowed for distributed communication that laid the foundation for the internet.

ARPANET's success demonstrated the power of packet-switching technology and provided the technical framework for what would later become the global internet. It also introduced TCP/IP, the standard protocol suite that is still in use today.

TCP/IP and the Global Network

  • What: Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol (TCP/IP) is a set of communication protocols used to interconnect network devices on the internet.
  • When: TCP/IP was adopted as the standard protocol for ARPANET in 1983, marking a crucial step in creating the internet as we know it.
  • Why: TCP/IP was designed to enable reliable communication between different networks, making it the standard that enabled the internet to scale globally.
  • Who: Vint Cerf and Bob Kahn are credited with developing TCP/IP, often referred to as the "fathers of the internet".
  • How: TCP/IP provided a common language for computers to communicate, allowing disparate networks to connect and form a larger, more resilient global network.

With the adoption of TCP/IP, networks could easily interconnect, allowing the internet to grow into the massive, interconnected web that we experience today. This protocol suite was fundamental in transforming a military project into a global communication phenomenon.

Birth of the World Wide Web

  • What: The World Wide Web (WWW) is an information system that allows documents to be connected via hyperlinks, accessible through the internet.
  • When: The WWW was invented in 1989 by Tim Berners-Lee and became publicly accessible in 1991.
  • Why: The purpose was to create a universal and accessible platform where information could be shared globally in a simple and intuitive way.
  • Who: Tim Berners-Lee, a British scientist, is credited with inventing the World Wide Web while working at CERN.
  • How: By combining hypertext with the TCP/IP protocol, Berners-Lee enabled users to access and navigate linked information on the internet.

The World Wide Web transformed the internet from a technical infrastructure used by scientists and academics into a platform accessible to the public. It enabled the sharing of information, communication, and commerce, ultimately changing how society functions.
